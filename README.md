Spatial autocorrelation of species diversity and distributions in time
and across spatial scales
================

## Data and R code repository

This repository
(<https://github.com/carmensoria/sac-diversity-distributions>) contains
the **data** and **R code** for our study.

Spatial autocorrelation can be a metric of aggregation or fragmentation
of spatial distributions. In this study, we used four temporally
replicated bird atlases (Czech Republic, Europe, sufficient to calculate
the SAC of species diversity and distributions, as well as to perform
the associated statistical analyses of how it changes across time and
spatial grain, and to assessNew York State, Japan), each aggregated to
multiple spatial grains, and calculated the spatial autocorrelation
(SAC) of individual species and richness. We then quantified the
magnitude, spatial scaling, and temporal change of SAC in both species
distributions and richness across the four regions.

NOTE: We do not include the raw breeding bird atlas data. The data and
code we provide are enough to calculate the SAC of species diversity and
distributions, as well as the associated statistical analysis on how it
changes across time and spatial grain, as well as its relationship with
species traits. The European bird data can be requested
[here](https://ebba2.info/data-request/), Czech bird data can be
requested from Karel Šťastný and Vladimír Bejček (authors of the
Breeding Birds Atlases of the Czech Republic), Japanese birds data can
be requested from the Japan Bird Research Association or accessed
[here](https://ikilog.biodic.go.jp), and the New York birds data are
available
[here](https://data.ny.gov/Energy-Environment/Breeding-Bird-Atlases/vk8g-ypxi).
Data on species traits are openly available from AVONET (Tobias *et
al.*, [2022](https://doi.org/10.1111/ele.13898)).
